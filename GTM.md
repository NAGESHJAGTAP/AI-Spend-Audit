# AI Spend Audit - Go-To-Market (GTM) Plan

This document details the targeting, search queries, digital communities, launch tactics, and weekly traction metrics for the AI Spend Audit tool.

---

## 1. The Exact Target User
Our target customer is the **Founding Engineer** or **Operations Lead** (e.g., Head of Ops/Finance) at an early-stage startup (Seed to Series A, team size 5 to 30). 

At this stage, they are growing fast, purchasing licenses dynamically (each developer signs up for Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT Pro on their corporate cards), and they do not have a dedicated procurement officer. The responsibility falls on the ops manager or founder, who sighs during the monthly credit card reconciliation but lacks the time to manually audit every tool account.

---

## 2. Pre-Purchase Search & Scroll Intent
Directly before searching for our solution, the target user is:
1. **Google Searching**:
   - *"How to manage startup SaaS spend"*
   - *"Redundant developer tools Cursor vs Copilot"*
   - *"Claude Team plan seat minimum pricing"*
   - *"How to optimize OpenAI API costs"*
2. **Scrolling on Social**:
   - Complaining on X (formerly Twitter) about Claude API rate limits or invoice spikes.
   - Reading Hacker News threads on the release of cheaper models (e.g. Gemini 1.5 Flash or GPT-4o-mini) and wondering how to migrate.

---

## 3. Digital Hangouts
We will distribute the tool directly where these professionals share knowledge:
- **Subreddits**: `/r/startup`, `/r/saas`, `/r/ycombinator`, `/r/devops`
- **Slack & Discord Networks**: Indie Hackers, YC Founder Slack (if accessible), Launch House, and tech communities like Dev.to and Hashnode.
- **X (Twitter)**: Target lists of bootstrap founders, SaaS builders, and fractional CFOs who specialize in tech stack optimization.

---

## 4. Path to First 100 Users (30 Days, $0 Budget)
We will secure our first 100 users through the following hyper-specific tactics:

1. **The "Complaining Founder" Scraping (Days 1-10)**:
   - Search X daily for the terms *"Claude bill"*, *"OpenAI spend"*, *"SaaS pricing"* or *"Cursor pricing"*. 
   - Reply to founders with a personalized comment: *"Hey, built a free tool that checks if your team is double-spending on Cursor + Copilot or ChatGPT + Claude. Takes 30s, no login. [link]"*
2. **Launch on Launch YC / Hacker News Show HN (Days 11-15)**:
   - Submit the tool as a "Show HN" on Hacker News with a transparent title: *"Show HN: An open-source Mint for startup AI subscription spend."* This community loves developer efficiency tools.
3. **Founder DM Campaign (Days 16-30)**:
   - DM 15 founders per day in YC directory or indie hacker Slacks. Offer them a free audit report in exchange for 5 minutes of UX feedback.

---

## 5. The Unfair Distribution Channel
Our unfair channel is **Fractional CFOs and Startup Bookkeepers**. 

We will form partnerships with fractional CFO agencies (like *Kruze Consulting* or *Pilot*) who manage bookkeeping for hundreds of Seed startups. We will give them co-branded access to the tool so they can run spend audits for their clients as a value-add. Each report they run showcases Techvruk as the automation implementation partner, giving us highly qualified leads.

---

## 6. Week-1 Traction Success Indicators
If the launch succeeds, we look for the following specific indicators of traction:
- **Audits Completed**: 150+
- **Emails Captured (Leads)**: 30+ (20% conversion rate)
- **High-Savings Leads (> $500/mo)**: 5+
- **Public Share Links Generated**: 15+
